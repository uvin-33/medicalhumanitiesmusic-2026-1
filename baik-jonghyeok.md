# joker

In the film *Joker*(2019), directed by Todd Philip Bunzl, music serves as a device to starkly contrast the protagonist's mental breakdown with his sense of liberation. In particular, the heavy, slow cello melody (Hildur Guðnadóttir's score) represents the depression and deep inner sorrow Arthur experiences, whereas the exciting beat of gary glitter's ['Rock and Roll Part 2'] (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BuyFhOIA-bE&list=RDBuyFhOIA-bE&start_radio=1) in the staircase scene paradoxically glorifies his mental rampage. When he, who had been suffering from pathological laughter, no longer suppresses his laughter and embraces the madness itself, the music shifts from a tragic funeral march to an upbeat celebratory anthem, effectively visualizing the chilling process of the illness transforming into criminal charisma. The movie *joker* can be thought of as just a provocative commercial film, but it is a work that sharply accuses modern medicine and social systems of neglecting and alienating "patients as humans." The mental suffering experienced by the protagonist, Arthur Fleck, is not just a biomedical "disease," but develops as an experience of subjective illness that is completely excluded under the pressure of social normality and normality. When analyzed by Arthur Frank's disease narrative framework, the heavy and slow cello score composed by Hildur Guðnadóttir in the first half of the film can be seen as an expression of an auditory chaos narrative that contains pain and isolation that cannot be verbalized. Physical and mental pain that is difficult to objectify with words resonates with the patient's inner truth through the monologue of the cello, a non-verbal medium. Gary Glitter's "Rock an Roll Part 2," on the other hand, shows the bizarre twist of the narrative as Arthur completely rejects non-disabled-centered norms and flows in the runaway stair scene. This is not a transition to a "overcoming narrative" that overcomes diseases or a normal "exploration narrative" that realizes the meaning of life. It is a devastating sense of liberation that projects one's own chaos into the world by breaking down the physical control of "health" and "rightness" defined by society with violence. At this moment when music turns from a tragic long song to a cheerful wedding song, the movie asks listeners a heavy question: will he become an ethical witness who accepts his destruction beyond just the audience.

# Music I hope to play at my funeral
The music I hope to be played at my funeral is Chopin Piano Sonata No. 2 3rd mov."Funeral March". I hope that the funeral will listen to music for those left behind because death is the last in my life that I will face someday. Those who will be saddened by my death may grieve again by recalling the funeral from their memories. If famous or special music is played at my funeral, my funeral will remain in people's memory better, so I hope that common music will play at my funeral so that those who are left behind won't have to grieve for a long time. Therefore, I chose Chopin Piano Sonata No. 2 3rd mov, which is commonly played at funerals.

# 내 장례식에서 연주되길 희망하는 음악
내 장례식에서 연주되길 희망하는 음악은 쇼팽 피아노 소나타 2번 3악장 '장송 행진곡'이다. 내가 생각하기에 죽음이라는 것은 모두 언젠가는 맞이하게될 삶의 마지막이기 때문에 장례식에서는 남겨진 사람들을 위한 음악이 들리면 좋겠다. 나의 죽음으로 인해 슬퍼할 사람들은 살아가면서 장례식을 기억에서 떠올리며 다시 슬퍼할지도 모른다. 만약 내 장례식에서 유명하거나 특별한 음악이 흘러나오면 그 장례식은 사람들의 기억에 더 잘 남아있을 것이기 때문에 내 장례식에서는 음악이라도 흔한 음악이 흘러나와 장례식이 남겨진 사람들이 오래 슬퍼할 일이 없게 만들면 좋겠다. 따라서 장례식에서 흔히 연주되는 음악인 쇼팽 피아노 소나타 2번 3악장을 골랐다.
